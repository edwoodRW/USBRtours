Revolution Square

Once known as Trafalgar Square, Revolution Square now stands as a symbol of the totalist revolution. The column is now adorned with the statue of Tony Blair, the leader who guided the revolution to its triumph.

People's Tower

Formerly the iconic Big Ben, the People's Tower now houses the main offices of the Totalist Party. The clock face has been replaced with the emblem of the party, and it chimes revolutionary anthems every hour.

Unity Bridge

Unity Bridge, previously known as the Tower Bridge, symbolizes the unity and strength of the people under the totalist regime. It is adorned with murals depicting scenes from the revolution and the rise of the People's Government.

Worker's Palace

The Buckingham Palace has been transformed into the Worker's Palace, a museum dedicated to the history of the working-class struggle and the achievements of the totalist state. It offers guided tours that delve into the revolution's history.

Liberty Hall

Formerly the Houses of Parliament, Liberty Hall now serves as the headquarters for the People's Assembly. The building is open to visitors who wish to learn about the new governance structure and the legislative process.

People's Park

Hyde Park has been reimagined as People's Park, a recreational area celebrating the revolution. It features statues of revolutionary heroes, spaces for public gatherings, and an annual festival celebrating the founding of the USBR.
